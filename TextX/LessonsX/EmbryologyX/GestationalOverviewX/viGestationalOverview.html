Gestational Overview


Description of Demonstration
This lesson is a continuously running video of the process of human gestation, from ovulation to the pre-term neonate.
Here is a list of the main events and structure that are depicted.
Ovulation
Fertilization
First Division of the zygote
Cleavages to the 8-cell morula
Compaction
Blastocyst with blastocoel, inner Cell Mass and Trophoblast
Hatching
Implantation
Syncytiotrophoblast and Cytotrophoblast
Lamination of inner cell mass into epiblast and hypoblast
Amnion expansion around the upper blastocoel to form the amniotic cavity
Expansion of hypoblast around lower blastocoel to form the extraembryonic endoderm around the primary yolk sac
Differentiation of the extraembryonic mesoderm from the extraembryonic endoderm, between the cytotrophoblast and extraembryonic endoderm.
Formation of extraembryonic lacunae in the extraembryonic mesoderm and trophoblastic lacunae within the syncytiotrophoblast
Expansion of and coalescence of extraembryonic lacunae to form the chorionic cavity
The chorion consisting of syncytiotrophoblast, cytotrophoblast and somatic extraembryonic mesoderm,
The connecting stalk connecting the embryo and chorion.
Segregation of exocoelomic cyst from primary yolk sac, leaving the secondary yolk sac.
Morphogenesis of epiblast into trilaminar embryo.
Chorionic villi formation within chorion
Expansion of embryonic circulatory system within embryonic and extraembryonic mesoderm.
Heart formation
Morphogenesis of the embryo into the fetus with a head, face and limbs
Expansion of embryo and chorionic villi, with the embryo growing into the amniotic cavity and the villi remaining attached to the endometrium to for the placenta.
